Welcome to Ho Chi Minh City

Ho Chi Minh City, formerly known as Saigon, is the largest city in Vietnam. It is a busy metropolis known for its rich history and its blend in modern and traditional culture.

Interesting Facts

  • Populous: Largest city in Vietnam.
  • Yellow Light: You see a yellow light, drive faster. It's the norm.
  • French Colonial Architecture: The city boasts well-preserved French colonial landmarks.
  • Cu Chi Tunnels: A network of tunnels used during the war, now open for exploration.
  • Motorbike City: Ho Chi Minh City is often called the "Motorbike City."
  • Marketplaces: Explore vibrant markets like Ben Thanh Market.
  • Diverse Food: Enjoy a diverse array of street food, from pho to banh mi.
  • Cultural Traditions: The city is a melting pot of diverse traditions and cultures.
  • Name: The city is named after a famous revolutionary leader, Ho Chi Minh.

Why Ho Chi Minh City?

Ho Chi Minh City was named after Ho Chi Minh, the revolutionary leader who played a key role in Vietnam's struggle for independence from French colonial rule and the reunification of North and South Vietnam. He was a revered figure in Vietnamese history and a central figure in the country's fight for freedom and self-determination. The renaming of the city from Saigon to Ho Chi Minh City was intended to honor his contributions and legacy.
